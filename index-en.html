About Me

I am from Campinas-SP, where I was born and lived until I moved to São Paulo, where I attended college. I am passionate about music, which has always been and still is a part of my life. Being of Japanese descent, I have had a significant involvement in cultural activities of the Japanese community and developed a growing interest in the social and psychological issues that are relevant to the experiences of so many people in the Japanese community, both within Japan and abroad.

I hold a degree in Psychology from the Institute of Psychology at the University of São Paulo (IP-USP). During my college years, I had the privilege of being a teaching assistant and later an intern at the Laboratory of Studies in Existential Phenomenology at the University of São Paulo (LEFE-USP), where I had the opportunity to work in the psychological emergency service of several institutions, such as the University Hospital, the Legal Department of São Francisco College, among others.

I lived in Japan for four and a half years, where I worked with children and teenagers, teaching Psychology, emotional education, and music. During my time in Japan, I continued my work as a psychologist online, and soon found myself dedicated to providing care for Brazilians living abroad. I have experience in Psychological Counseling and Clinical Care for couples and individuals in both Portuguese and English.

FAQ

Psychotherapy is a process of self-knowledge and emotional treatment, recommended for anyone who wants to better understand their feelings, cope with challenges, or improve their quality of life.

The psychologist provides therapy through conversation and psychological techniques, while the psychiatrist is a doctor who can diagnose mental disorders and prescribe medications.

The duration varies depending on each person and their needs. Some issues can be addressed in a few sessions, while others may require longer-term support.
